Why a 10,000 mAh power bank does not charge your phone three times

7 July 20265 min readLYNX Engineering
LYNX // CHARGING 101 // 2026.07.07

The number on the box is real. It is just not measuring what most people assume it is measuring.

Buy a 10,000 mAh power bank, look at your phone's 4,000 mAh battery, and the arithmetic seems obvious: two and a half charges, maybe more. In practice you will get somewhere between one and a half and two. Nothing is wrong with the bank. The two numbers are simply measured at different voltages.

Where the capacity goes

A power bank's cells sit at about 3.7 V. Your phone charges over USB at 5 V or higher. Getting from one to the other requires a boost converter, and that conversion is where most of the gap appears.

Run the real numbers on a 10,000 mAh bank:

  • Stored energy: 10,000 mAh × 3.7 V = 37 Wh.
  • After conversion at roughly 90% efficiency: about 33 Wh reaches the cable.
  • Expressed at 5 V, that is around 6,600 mAh of usable output.

Then subtract the losses at the other end — heat in the phone's own charging circuit, the screen you keep checking, background apps — and a realistic figure is 60 to 70% of the rated number. That is not a defect. It is physics, and it applies to every brand equally.

Quick estimateMultiply the rated mAh by 0.65, then divide by your phone's battery capacity. A 10,000 mAh bank and a 4,000 mAh phone: 6,500 ÷ 4,000 = roughly 1.6 full charges.

Wireless costs more than wired

Magnetic wireless charging trades a little efficiency for the convenience of no cable. Energy crossing an air gap between two coils runs warmer than energy travelling down a wire, so expect roughly 10 to 15% less delivered capacity than the same bank charging over Type-C.

Whether that matters depends entirely on the situation. Topping up while walking through an airport, the convenience is worth more than the percentage. Charging overnight from a low bank, plug the cable in.

Choosing a size that fits the job

Once you adjust for real-world output, capacity choices get clearer. A 5,000 mAh bank delivers roughly one full phone charge — the right size for a day out, a commute, or a flight, at a weight you will not notice. A 10,000 mAh bank delivers close to two, which covers a weekend away or a long day of shooting photos.

Beyond that, you are mostly buying weight. The extra capacity is genuine, but so are the extra grams in your pocket every single day, for a reserve you use a handful of times a year.

Common questions

Is my power bank faulty if it gives fewer charges than advertised?

Almost certainly not. A 30 to 40% gap between rated and delivered capacity is normal and expected across all brands, because of voltage conversion losses.

What does mAh actually measure?

Milliamp-hours measure charge at the cell's own voltage, usually 3.7 V. Watt-hours measure energy and are directly comparable between devices.

Does wireless charging waste a lot of power?

It is roughly 10 to 15% less efficient than charging by cable. Meaningful over a full cycle, negligible for a quick top-up.
